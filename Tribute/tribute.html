Women's History Month - Grace Hopper

Computer pioneer, naval officer and mathematician who helped develop programming languages and the first computer compiler.

Here is a time line of Grace's life and accomplishments:

    1906 - Born on December 9, in New York City, New York.

    1928 - Graduated from Vassar College with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Mathematics and Physics.

    1930 - Earned a Master's degree in Mathematics from Yale University.

    1934 - Received a Ph.D. in Mathematics from Yale University, becoming one of the first women to earn a doctorate in mathematics from the institution.

    1943 - Joined the U.S. Naval Reserve (Womenâ€™s Reserve) during World War II, where she was assigned to the Bureau of Ships Computation Project at Harvard University.

    1944 - Worked on the Mark I computer, one of the first electromechanical computers, and was involved in programming its operations.

    1949 - Developed the first compiler (A-0 System) for computer programming, which translated mathematical codes into machine language.

    1952 - Coined the term "debugging" after removing a moth from a computer, which has since become a standard term in computer science.

    1959 - Played a key role in the development of COBOL (Common Business-Oriented Language), one of the first high-level programming languages.

    1966 - Became the first woman to be named a Distinguished Fellow of the British Computer Society.

    1973 - Retired from the Navy as a Rear Admiral, one of the first women to achieve that rank.

    1991 - Passed away on January 1, in Arlington, Virginia. She is remembered as a pioneer in computer programming and an advocate for women in technology.
